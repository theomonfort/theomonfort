**Macro Trends and Emerging (Relevant) Tech**
_There is a robust ecosystem around GitHub that impacts our positioning and traction. This section brings select developments and pertinent analysis of technologies in our sphere._ 

* A busy earnings season continues: 
  * **ASML**, a supplier of advanced chip manufacturing equipment, [reported Q4FY25 revenue of €9.72Bn](https://www.asml.com/en/investors/financial-results/q4-2025) (+5% YoY). However, ASML highlighted FY26 sales of between €34 and €39Bn, driven by AI-related demand. 
  * **AMD** [reported Q4FY25 revenue of $10.3Bn](https://ir.amd.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1276/amd-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2025-financial-results) (+34% YoY), with net profit of $1.5Bn (+213% YoY). Growth was driven by data center demand for AMD GPUs. 
  * **Intel** reported [Q4FY25 revenue of $13.7M](https://www.intc.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1759/intel-reports-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2025-financial) (-4% YoY), with a net loss of $333M. Intel is struggling to meet data center demand, having reduced manufacturing capacity. 
  * **Samsung** [reported Q4FY25 revenue of $65.6Bn (+24% YoY),](https://news.samsung.com/global/samsung-electronics-announces-fourth-quarter-and-fy-2025-results) with net profit of $13.7Bn (+61% YoY), driven by demand for AI related components. Samsung has addressed earlier issues with quality and delivery (see Mona Monitor [19](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/26))
  * **IBM** [announced Q4FY25 revenue of $19.7Bn](https://www.ibm.com/downloads/documents/us-en/1550f7eea8c0ded6) (+12% YoY). IBM cited strong GenAI-related growth.  
  * **Apple** reported [Q1FY26 revenue of $143.8Bn](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2026/01/apple-reports-first-quarter-results/) (+16% YoY) driven by a record quarterly sale of iPhones. 
  * **Meta** reported [Q4FY25 revenue of $59.9Bn](https://investor.atmeta.com/investor-news/press-release-details/2026/Meta-Reports-Fourth-Quarter-and-Full-Year-2025-Results/default.aspx) (+24% YoY). Meta plan to expand their overall capex spend for 2026 to $135Bn - matching projected free cashflow. 
 
* **SpaceX** [acquired **xAI**](https://www.spacex.com/updates#xai-joins-spacex) at a valuation of $250Bn, [notably higher than Anthropic's recent $183Bn valuation](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-raises-series-f-at-usd183b-post-money-valuation). The Economist, a leading business publication, [published commentary](https://www.economist.com/business/2026/02/03/elon-musks-mega-merger-makes-little-business-sense) on this remarkable piece of financial engineering. 

* **OpenClaw**, a personal AI assistant, grew extremely rapidly ([introduction post](https://openclaw.ai/blog/introducing-openclaw) – note this url is blocked on GitHub laptops). Various vendors latched onto OpenClaw immediately for marketing purposes with [**Docker**](https://www.docker.com/blog/clawdbot-docker-model-runner-private-personal-ai/) and [**Cloudflare**](https://blog.cloudflare.com/moltworker-self-hosted-ai-agent/) showing how to host agents, while security vendors including [**GitGuardian**](https://blog.gitguardian.com/moltbot-personal-assistant-goes-viral-and-so-do-your-secrets/), [**Lasso**](https://www.lasso.security/blog/openclaw-discovery-shadow-ai-security) and [**jFrog**](https://jfrog.com/blog/giving-openclaw-the-keys-to-your-kingdom-read-this-first/) all went down the risk to secrets route.

**Competitor News**
_This section covers select updates and analysis from known and emerging competitors._  

**AI**

* **OpenAI** [released a new desktop app, once again called Codex](https://openai.com/index/introducing-the-codex-app/), which allows users to run and monitor multiple agents in parallel.

* **Augment Code** [announced a research preview of Intent](https://www.augmentcode.com/blog/the-end-of-linear-work), a new agent orchestration and spec driven development collaboration tool. 

* **Apple** and **Anthropic** [announced direct support for Claude within XCode](https://www.anthropic.com/news/apple-xcode-claude-agent-sdk) using the claude-agent-sdk. 

* **Cline** [announced Cline CLI 2.0](https://cline.bot/blog/announcing-cline-cli-2-0), positioned as an opensource coding-agent CLI. Cline has surpassed 5M installs across IDE platforms, although as with any numbers stated like this you should note that installs do not equal users. Cline has [222 releases](https://github.com/cline/cline/releases) at the time of writing. 

* **Cursor** [released version 2.4](https://cursor.com/changelog/2-4), including subagents and a new enterprise only feature Cursor Blame ([docs](https://cursor.com/docs/integrations/cursor-blame)), which provides a line by line breakdown of who, model or human, contributed each line of code with full attribution. Cursor Blame builds on [**Agent Trace**](https://agent-trace.dev/), a new standard released by Cursor, along with other vendors including **Cline**, **Cognition**, **Google**, **Vercel** and **Cloudflare**. Other vendors will add similar functionality soon. 

* **Windsurf**, an AI IDE from **Cognition**, [released a new version incorporating an “arena mode”](https://windsurf.com/blog/windsurf-wave-14) which allows users to compare two models side by side.  

* **KiloCode**, an opensource AI Coding Assistant founded by the former CEO of GitLab, [announced version 1.0 of the Kilo Code CLI](https://blog.kilo.ai/p/kilo-cli). 

* **Mistral**, a frontier model lab, [announced mistral-vibe](https://mistral.ai/news/mistral-vibe-2-0), an updated CLI which seems to be intent on sowing messaging confusion with technical and vibe coding users alike. 

* **TabNine**, a provider of on-premises AI Coding Assistants, [released the Tabnine CLI](https://www.tabnine.com/blog/introducing-the-tabnine-cli/). 

* **Cognition**, the maker of Devin and Windsurf, [announced Devin Review](https://cognition.ai/blog/devin-review), an AI code review tool. 

* **Qodo**, an AI Code Review vendor, [introduced Qodo 2.0](https://www.qodo.ai/blog/introducing-qodo-2-0-agentic-code-review/). Qodo are positioning this release as a next-gen code review. Under the hood, Qodo is using a multi agent approach for code review, in a style similar to [Karpathy’s LLM council](https://github.com/karpathy/llm-council). 

* **CodeRabbit**, an AI Code Review vendor, [released a user management API.](https://docs.coderabbit.ai/changelog#january-21%2C-2026) 

* **Atlassian** started pushing Rovo Dev as a code review tool, [with a thought leadership piece on how they reduced PR cycle times](https://www.atlassian.com/blog/announcements/how-we-cut-pr-cycle-time-with-ai-code-reviews). 

* **Baz**, a code review vendor, [introduced a beta feature: Baz Fixer](https://baz.co/resources/fixer). Baz Fixer spins up a sandbox environment to implement fixes from the Baz code review tool. 

* **Tessl** [announced a package manager for agent skills](https://tessl.io/blog/skills-are-software-and-they-need-a-lifecycle-introducing-skills-on-tessl/) 

* **Google** and **Deeplearning.AI** [launched a course on using gemini-cli.](https://cloud.google.com/blog/topics/developers-practitioners/mastering-gemini-cli-your-complete-guide-from-installation-to-advanced-use-cases/)

* **Inferact**, a company formed around the vLLM opensource project, [announced a $150M Series A](https://inferact.ai/) led by A16Z and Lightspeed Ventures.


**DevOps**

* Version control system projects are back in vogue, albeit with an agentic flavor. **Atomic.dev** [announced a $2.5M Pre-Seed round](https://x.com/atomicdotdev/status/2014004254927651178), while opensource project [**Lix**](https://lix.dev/blog/introducing-lix) found some [interest](https://github.slack.com/archives/C1YRT029W/p1769698302798019) [slack thread] within GitHub. 

* **Cloudbees** [announced the appointment of Philippe Van Hove as CRO](https://www.cloudbees.com/blog/cloudbees-appoints-philippe-van-hove-chief-revenue-officer). 

* **Atlassian** [announced CPU and memory upgrades for BitBucket Pipelines](https://www.atlassian.com/blog/bitbucket/power-up-your-pipeline-workflow-with-24x-and-32x-step-sizes). 

* Sandboxes for running AI Agents continue to make waves, with **Docker** [announcing a sandbox for running Claude Code](https://www.docker.com/blog/docker-sandboxes-run-claude-code-and-other-coding-agents-unsupervised-but-safely/) (see Mona Monitor [44](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/55)) and other tools, and **Vercel** [announcing the GA of their microVM based offering](https://vercel.com/blog/vercel-sandbox-is-now-generally-available). 

**Security**

* **Rapidfort**, a container security vendor, [announced a $42M Series A](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260203036969/en/RapidFort-Raises-%2442M-Series-A-Led-by-Blue-Cloud-Ventures-and-Forgepoint-Capital-to-Power-the-Next-Generation-of-Software-Supply-Chain-Defense), led by Blue Point Ventures and Forgecapital. Rapidfort is focusing on automated remediation for software supply chains.

**Data Points and Other**

* **Sonatype** released their [2026 State of the Software Supply Chain report](https://www.sonatype.com/state-of-the-software-supply-chain/introduction), with a heavy focus on Open-Source related vulnerabilities.  

* Analyst firm **RedMonk** [published a piece on the sometimes negative impact of AI on Open-Source contributions](https://redmonk.com/kholterhoff/2026/02/03/ai-slopageddon-and-the-oss-maintainers/). This is already [an area of focus for GitHub.](https://github.com/orgs/community/discussions/185387)

* **JetBrains** [released their annual State of Game Development report](https://lp.jetbrains.com/the-state-of-gamedev-2025/). Perforce remain the preferred SCM vendor in the space. ChatGPT (59%) and GitHub Copilot (53%) are the preferred AI tools in professional studios; GitHub Actions (29%) leads in CI, and C# (40%) remains the dominant programming language.   

* The results of the [2025 Go Developer Survey](https://go.dev/blog/survey2025) were released. Lots of data points, but most directly relevant for GitHub is the stat on AI tools used, with ChatGPT (45%), GitHub Copilot (31%) and Claude Code (25%) as the leaders. 

* **SonarSource** [released another positioning piece on Shadow AI use.](https://www.sonarsource.com/blog/shadow-ai-is-already-writing-your-code/) Similar to the reports above GitHub Copilot (75%) and ChatGPT (74%) are the leading AI tools in the data cited.

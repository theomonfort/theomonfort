**Macro Trends and Emerging (Relevant) Tech**
_There is a robust ecosystem around GitHub that impacts our positioning and traction. This section brings select developments and pertinent analysis of technologies in our sphere._ 

* **Oracle** [announced Q2FY26 earnings](https://s23.q4cdn.com/440135859/files/doc_earnings/2026/q2/earnings-result/2q26-pressrelease-final.pdf) of $14Bn (+14% YoY). **Broadcom**, a chipmaker and software conglomerate, [announced Q4FY25 earnings](https://investors.broadcom.com/news-releases/news-release-details/broadcom-inc-announces-fourth-quarter-and-fiscal-year-2025) of $18Bn (+28% YoY.  Oracle and Broadcom saw significant declines in their stock as investors weighed up the implications of spending commitments made by, and exposure to, **OpenAI**. **Synopsys**, a provider of chip design software, [announced Q4FY25 earnings](https://investor.synopsys.com/news/news-details/2025/Synopsys-Posts-Financial-Results-for-Fourth-Quarter-and-Fiscal-Year-2025/default.aspx) of $2.3Bn (+37% YoY). **Salesforce**, a CRM company, [announced Q3FY26](https://s205.q4cdn.com/626266368/files/doc_financials/2026/q3/CRM-Q3-FY26-Earnings-Press-Release.pdf) earnings of $10.3Bn (+9% YoY).
 
* Acquisitions filled the news. **Nvidia** [acquired **SchedMD**](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/nvidia-acquires-schedmd/), the makers of Slurm, a workload management system. **OpenAI** [acquired **Neptune**](https://openai.com/index/openai-to-acquire-neptune/), an experiment management and visibility tool used in model development.  **IBM** [acquired **Confluent**](https://newsroom.ibm.com/2025-12-08-ibm-to-acquire-confluent-to-create-smart-data-platform-for-enterprise-generative-ai),  the originators of Apache Kafka. **Marvel**, a chip manufacturer, [acquired **Celestial AI**](https://investor.marvell.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1000/marvell-to-acquire-celestial-ai-accelerating-scale-up-connectivity-for-next-generation-data-centers), a maker of optical networking equipment used within datacenters. 


* The **Agentic AI Foundation** was [launched](https://www.linuxfoundation.org/press/linux-foundation-announces-the-formation-of-the-agentic-ai-foundation) by the **Linux Foundation** and is now the home for [MCP](https://github.com/modelcontextprotocol) and [Agents.md](http://agents.md/), with more projects to come. Founding members include [**GitHub**](https://github.blog/open-source/maintainers/mcp-joins-the-linux-foundation-what-this-means-for-developers-building-the-next-era-of-ai-tools-and-agents/), [**OpenAI**](https://openai.com/index/agentic-ai-foundation/). [**Anthropic**](https://www.anthropic.com/news/donating-the-model-context-protocol-and-establishing-of-the-agentic-ai-foundation),  [**JetBrains**](https://blog.jetbrains.com/blog/2025/12/09/jetbrains-joins-the-agentic-artificial-intelligence-foundation/), [**Docker**](https://www.docker.com/blog/docker-joins-the-agentic-ai-foundation/) and a host of other Linux Foundation regulars. 

* **OpenAI** [appointed Denise Dresser as CRO](https://openai.com/index/openai-appoints-denise-dresser/). Dresser was previously CEO of **Slack** (itself a division of **Salesforce**) and a leader in Salesforce's enterprise sales organisation. Ashley Kramer, [who left **GitLab** last year after not getting the CEO job](https://docs.google.com/document/d/13FH6z4LErBv73LdTYLdiaAJrTZoucS8JGAl5Oxw0gYQ/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.dwevbe7kdyr0), has changed her title to Enterprise Sales Leader, having previously been CRO at OpenAI. 

* **Amazon** [announced a new AI and chips focused division](https://www.aboutamazon.com/news/company-news/andy-jassy-peter-desantis-amazon-leadership-update) which will be run by long time AWS veteran Peter de Santis. This is an entirely new division and appears to split out frontier model research and chip design from AWS.

**Competitor News**
_This section covers select updates and analysis from known and emerging competitors._  

**AI**

* **Cursor** released [version 2.2](https://cursor.com/changelog/2-2) of their IDE. They highlighted two new features: [debug mode](https://cursor.com/blog/debug-mode), a way to instrument your code with various hypotheses on what an issue might be, test again, and then start to fix and a[ visual editor](https://cursor.com/blog/browser-visual-editor) within Cursor. 

* **Anthropic** made two significant updates to Claude Code, [a slack integration](https://www.claude.com/blog/claude-code-and-slack) and quietly [introducing async background agents](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/blob/main/CHANGELOG.md#2064). We should expect marketing beyond  the async [social posts](https://x.com/claudeai/status/1998830338735485239) in the new year. Claude also [introduced beta support for controlling a web browser via a chrome extension](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/blob/main/CHANGELOG.md#2072). 

* Frontier model providers released three new high profile models that are used with AI Code Assistants. Influencers rushed to tell us why each new model is the greatest model since the last greatest model.
   * **OpenAI** [released GPT 5.2](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-2/) As is now customary, support announcements came out from multiple tool providers, including [**GitHub**](https://github.blog/changelog/2025-12-11-openais-gpt-5-2-is-now-in-public-preview-for-github-copilot/), [**Windsurf**](https://windsurf.com/blog/gpt-5-2), [**Cursor**](https://x.com/cursor_ai/status/1999183968776626230?s=20), [**Cline**](https://cline.bot/blog/cline-v3-41-adds-gpt-5-2-devstral-2-ergonomic-model-switching). 
  * **Google** released Gemini 3 Flash. As with the OpenAI GPT 5.2 release, multiple tool providers, including [**GitHub**](https://github.blog/changelog/2025-12-17-gemini-3-flash-is-now-in-public-preview-for-github-copilot/), [**Amp**](https://ampcode.com/news/finder-gemini3-flash) and  [**Cursor**](https://x.com/cursor_ai/status/2001326908030804293?s=20) immediately announced support. Gemini 3 Flash was also made available in [Google Antigravity](https://antigravity.google/blog/gemini-3-flash-in-google-antigravity) and the [Gemini CLI](https://developers.googleblog.com/gemini-3-flash-is-now-available-in-gemini-cli/). 
  * **Mistral**, a frontier model provider, [released DevStral 2](https://mistral.ai/news/devstral-2-vibe-cli), their latest coding model along with a CLI. Mistral does not benefit from the coolness factor of multiple changelog updates. 
  * **Cursor** no longer does major announcements for new models, preferring social and a documentation update. This reflects an assumption of their user base that they will always bring new models into the platform. 

* **Kilo Code**, founded by former GitLab CEO Sid Sijbrandij, [announced an $8M seed round](https://blog.kilo.ai/p/kilo-raised-8-million-seed-round), led by Cota Capital. Kilo code is positioned as an "Agentic Engineering Platform”. Kilo Code also [announced cloud agents](https://blog.kilo.ai/p/cloud-agents), an isolated cloud environment for an agent to run in. Kilo Code has chosen to use containers as the underlying infrastructure layer.   

* **Sonar**, a code quality vendor, has [introduced a new Architecture feature to SonarQube.](https://www.sonarsource.com/blog/introducing-architecture-in-sonarqube/) The feature allows teams to document architectures and prevent drift. The feature is a closed beta, but [documentation](https://docs.sonarsource.com/sonarqube-cloud/architecture) is available. 

* **Augment Code**, an AI development tools vendor, [introduced a new code review feature](https://www.augmentcode.com/blog/introducing-augment-code-review). 

* **CodeRabbit**, an AI Code Review Vendor, [released an analysis of AI code in OpenSource projects](https://www.coderabbit.ai/blog/state-of-ai-vs-human-code-generation-report) stating that AI generated code introduced 1.7 times more issues vs human created code. Readers will be unsurprised to learn the CodeRabbit sells a solution to this problem. 

* **JetBrains** [are moving Junie](https://blog.jetbrains.com/ai/2025/12/junie-now-integrated-into-the-ai-chat/), their AI Coding Agent, (see Mona Monitor [19](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/26)) into their main AI Chat and are deprecating the standalone plugin.  

* **ZenCoder** [released ZenFlow](https://zencoder.ai/blog/ai-orchestration-explained-the-system-behind-high-quality-ai-output), a new AI Agent Orchestration engine. 

* **Cline**, an AI Coding Agent, has [moved from **OpenRouter** to the **Vercel** API Gateway](https://cline.bot/blog/cline-provider-now-runs-on-vercel). This is a significant moment for OpenRouter as Cline have historically relied on OpenRouter stats as a marketing vehicle. 

* **Netlify** [announced their own AI Gateway](https://www.netlify.com/blog/ai-gateway-is-now-generally-available/) is generally available. The AI Gateway offering is critical as they compete with **Vercel**. 

* **Replit**, a vibe coding vendor, and **Google** brought some positive vibes to the enterprise with [an updated partnership](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/bringing-vibe-coding-to-the-enterprise-with-replit/). The announcement mainly focuses on support for models, including Gemini 3, in Replit and GTM programs via Google Cloud Marketplace. Vibe++. 

* **Lovable**, a vibe coding vendor, [introduced a plan mode to its product](https://lovable.dev/blog/chat-mode-and-questions). In a pattern which will be familiar to practitioners of software engineering it appears that the one shot vibes may actually need some structure and design to become applications. 

* **Lovable** also [announced a $330M Series B raise](https://age-of-the-builder.lovable.app/). The vibes are good apparently.

* **Port**, an internal developer portal vendor, completed its rebrand as an AI Agent Portal with [a $100M Series C raise](https://www.port.io/blog/port-100m-series-c), led by General Atlantic.

**DevOps**

* **Harness** [announced a $240M Series E funding round](https://www.harness.io/blog/240m-financing-to-bring-ai-to-everything-after-code), led by **Goldman Sachs**. Harness is now valued at $5.5Bn. Harness continues to position itself as serving the rest of the SDLC beyond code generation. This is the same positioning as **GitLab** is using. 

* **Semaphore**, a CI/CD provider,  [announced the GA of their ARM offering](https://semaphore.io/blog/arm-machines)

**Security**

* **Checkmarx** [acquired **Tromzo**](https://checkmarx.com/blog/leading-the-shift-to-a-continuous-agentic-appsec-checkmarx-acquires-tromzo/), an ASPM platform. Tromzo had raised $11M. The Tromzo IP will be merged into the [Checkmarx Assist](https://checkmarx.com/product/checkmarx-one-assist/) offering. 

* **Wiz** [ nnounced a new SAST offering](https://www.wiz.io/blog/introducing-wiz-sast-where-code-risk-meets-cloud-context). 

* **Docker** continued defensive moves against **Chainguard** and **Echo Software** and [announced Docker Hardened Images are now available for free](https://www.docker.com/blog/docker-hardened-images-for-every-developer/). 

* **BlackDuck** [announced BlackDuck Signal](https://www.blackduck.com/blog/black-duck-signal-ai-application-security.html), an agentic AI solution to, in BlackDucks words, reduce the noise in application security. At its core this looks to be packaging up the BlackDuck knowledge base in a format for an LLM to use to discover and fix issues during the development cycle. The offering is early access only and no product docs are available.
 
* **TruffleHog**, a secret scanning vendor, introduced a new JWT detector and[ published a detailed blog on their initial findings](https://trufflesecurity.com/blog/trufflehog-now-detects-jwts-with-public-key-signatures-and-verifies-them-for-liveness). 

* **React2Shell**, a major vulnerability with React, reverberated around the internet, including indirectly [causing an outage for Cloudflare](https://blog.cloudflare.com/5-december-2025-outage/) due to mitigations they were putting in place in their WAF offering. Various vendors, including  [Sonatype](https://www.sonatype.com/blog/react2shell-rce-vulnerabilities-require-immediate-attention), [JFrog](https://jfrog.com/blog/2025-55182-and-2025-66478-react2shell-all-you-need-to-know/), [SemGrep](https://semgrep.dev/blog/2025/new-react2shell-offspring-patched-react-server-components-dos-and-source-code-exposure/). [SourceGraph](https://sourcegraph.com/blog/episode-3-revenge-of-the-react-vulnerabilities) and  [StackHawk](https://www.stackhawk.com/blog/react2shell-vulnerability-response/) wrote guidance and product positioning around the vulnerability. 

* **OWASP** released the [OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications](https://genai.owasp.org/resource/owasp-top-10-for-agentic-applications-for-2026/).

**Data Points and Other**

* **Cloudflare** [released their annual Cloudflare Radar](https://blog.cloudflare.com/radar-2025-year-in-review/). This report always has nuggets of valuable information given the amount of traffic Cloudflare observes.
  * React remains the top overall framework for building web interfaces
  * Go and Python are the two two languages for interacting with APIs 
  * Googlebot dwarfed all other bot crawlers combined. 

* **Greptile**, a code review vendor, released a [State of AI Coding](https://www.greptile.com/state-of-ai-coding-2025) report. There is an engineering velocity section, but there are no details on sample sizes, so it is hard to draw conclusions beyond marketing from the data. They argue developer output is up 76% based on lines of code as a metric. Lines of Code does not measure if any business objective has been achieved. 

* **Battery Ventures** released their bi-annual [State of Enterprise Tech Funding](https://www.battery.com/blog/survey-says-enterprises-shift-from-ai-pilots-to-production-and-agentic-systems-surge-ahead/) report. GitHub Copilot was highlighted as the leading tool used by those surveyed for AI Assisted Coding. Caution is needed on the report as they also show Tabnine being evaluated by 15% of respondents, which does not mirror any other data we are seeing. 

* **Open Router** [released a State of AI report](https://openrouter.ai/state-of-ai) based on the traffic patterns they are seeing. VC Tom Tunguz has done [an excellent analysis](https://www.tomtunguz.com/2025-12-16-open-router-insights/) on the results, highlighting that the split between proprietary and open source models remains around 75/25.  

* Learning platforms **Coursera** and **Udemy** [announced a merger](https://investor.coursera.com/news/news-details/2025/Coursera-to-Combine-with-Udemy-to-Empower-the-Global-Workforce-with-Skills-for-the-AI-Era/default.aspx).

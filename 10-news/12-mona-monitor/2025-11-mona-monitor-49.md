**Macro Trends and Emerging (Relevant) Tech**
_There is a robust ecosystem around GitHub that impacts our positioning and traction. This section brings select developments and pertinent analysis of technologies in our sphere._ 

* **Nvidia** [reported Q3FY26 earnings](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-announces-financial-results-for-third-quarter-fiscal-2026) of $57Bn (+62% YoY). Nvida stated "Compute demand keeps accelerating and compounding across training and inference".  

* **CloudFlare** [had a significant outage](https://blog.cloudflare.com/18-november-2025-outage/), disrupting substantial portions of the internet including services from [**OpenAI**](https://status.openai.com/incidents/01KABE2437NJYKBFHT22SD3H92) and [**Anthropic**](https://status.claude.com/incidents/p0svq4j6sk04). 

* **AMD** [acquired **MK1**,](https://www.amd.com/en/blogs/2025/amd-acquires-mk1-to-advance-ai-inference-performance.html) a maker of high speed inference technologies. Continuing on the inference team, **Google** [announced their latest TPU, Ironwood](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/compute/ironwood-tpus-and-new-axion-based-vms-for-your-ai-workloads/), which is focused on inference workloads. 

* Industry analysts who attended Universe shared their write ups: 
  * [**RedMonk**](https://redmonk.com/sogrady/2025/11/07/github-2025/) focused on our increased velocity in shipping and the value of the GitHub/Microsoft relationship. 
  * [**Forrester**](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/github-universe-2025-is-microsoft-eating-github-or-is-it-the-other-way-around/) went for spicy takes around CoreAI (Forrester's market share continues to [decline](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/carterlusher_archat-analystrelations-forrester-activity-7358152063123378176-dBU-/), which leads to more spicy takes). 
  * **IDC** [published a write up](https://my.idc.com/getdoc.jsp?containerId=lcUS53921325) focused on spec driven development and Copilot Metrics. We no longer have the relevant IDC subscription to access this report.

**Competitor News**
_This section covers select updates and analysis from known and emerging competitors._  

**AI**

* **Cursor** [raised a series D of $2.3Bn led by Accel](https://cursor.com/blog/series-d), Thrive and A16Z. They also disclosed they have surpassed $1Bn in ARR. Their announcement specifically highlighted their ongoing work developing  in-house models (see Mona Monitor [39](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/48), [23](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/31), [16](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/23)).

* **Google** released Gemini 3 (see below) and a new code editor Antigravity, which is derived from the Windsurf IP licensed in (see Mona Monitor [31](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/40)). Antigravity comes with free access to Gemini 3, as well as access to models from Anthropic. Gartner [recommended](https://www.gartner.com/document-reader/document/7196130) piloting Antigravity. [Slack thread with more details on Gartner recommendation](https://github.slack.com/archives/C1YRT029W/p1763542111282749)

* **Google** released [Code Wiki](https://developers.googleblog.com/en/introducing-code-wiki-accelerating-your-code-understanding/), a code documentation tool. This is similar to DeepWiki from **Cognition** (see Mona Monitor [25](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/33)) which is now offered as part of **Windsurf** (see Mona Monitor [34](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/43))

* **Google** [released Gemini 3](https://blog.google/products/gemini/gemini-3/), their latest LLM.  As is customary coding assistant vendors announced they were supporting the model as well, with [**Windsurf**](https://windsurf.com/changelog), [**Cursor**](https://x.com/cursor_ai/status/1990814174264381910?s=20) and [**GitHub**](https://github.blog/changelog/2025-11-18-gemini-3-pro-is-in-public-preview-for-github-copilot/) among those making announcements.  

* **OpenAI** released [GPT-5.1-codex-max](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-1-codex-max/). For now this is only available in OpenAI Codex (cli, ide extension and cloud). 

* **OpenAI** [released GPT-5.1](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-1-for-developers/). Similar to the release of Gemini 3 noted above, the customary flurry of we support this model announcements came out, including [**Windsurf**](https://windsurf.com/blog/gpt-5-1) and [**GitHub**](https://github.blog/changelog/2025-11-13-openais-gpt-5-1-gpt-5-1-codex-and-gpt-5-1-codex-mini-are-now-in-public-preview-for-github-copilot/). **Cursor** only updated their [list of supported models](https://cursor.com/docs/models). 

* **Amazon** [announced the GA of Kiro](https://kiro.dev/blog/general-availability/). Amazon also [released the Kiro CLI,](https://kiro.dev/blog/introducing-kiro-cli/) which is essentially the older Q CLI plus social login and [automatic model selection](https://kiro.dev/docs/chat/model-selection/).  The Q CLI is officially [rebranded to Kiro CLI.](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/amazonq/latest/qdeveloper-ug/doc-history.html) 

* **Zed**, an AI Code Editor vendor, [announced three “agent extensions”](https://zed.dev/blog/agent-extensions) for **Augment Code**, **OpenCode** and **StakPak**. These use Agent Client Protocol (ACP) to connect beneath the hood. 

* **Moonshot**, a Chinese frontier model lab, released the [Kimi-K2 Thinking model](https://moonshotai.github.io/Kimi-K2/thinking.html). They recently released Kimi-K2 Instruct (see Mona Monitor [35](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/44)) and [Kimi-CLI](https://github.com/MoonshotAI/kimi-cli). Kimi-cli also supports ACP).

* **Poolside**, a frontier model lab and [recently anointed datacenter builder](https://poolside.ai/blog/announcing-project-horizon), [acquired **Fern Labs**](https://poolside.ai/blog/fern-labs-acquisition). It is unclear precisely what Fern Labs do other than being forward deployed engineers focused on bringing frontier models within enterprise boundaries. 

* **Spectral**, the team behind [Scale Lang](https://scale-lang.com/), a cross compiler which allows you to use cuda target code on other architectures such as AMDs GPUs, raised a seed round of $6M led by [Costanoa Ventures](https://www.costanoa.vc/portfolio/spectral-compute) & Crucible Capital. 

* **WebFlow**, a widely used website generator, [released a vibe coding component](https://webflow.com/feature/code-gen) 

* **Lovable** announced [they passed $200M in ARR](https://lovable.dev/blog/one-year-of-lovable), up from $100M in July (see Mona Monitor [31](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/40)). As in previous announcements COGS was not mentioned. 

* **Milestone**, a software engineering intelligence vendor focused on AI Coding tools, [announced a $10M seed round](https://mstone.ai/blog/from-classroom-in-dublin-to-future-of-ai-engineering/), led by Heavybit. 

* **OpenHands**, an AI infrastructure and tooling company, based on the OpenHands open source project, [raised a series A of $18M](https://openhands.dev/blog/weve-just-raised-18-8m-to-build-the-open-standard-for-autonomous-software-development) led by Madrona. 

* **HuggingFace** and **Google** [announced a new partnership](https://huggingface.co/blog/google-cloud). This appears to be predominantly focused on engineering and product features, with a new CDN Gateway being developed and an expansion of existing inference points (see Mona Monitor [5](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/10))

* **Vercel** [released a sandbox cli](https://vercel.com/changelog/vercel-sandbox-cli-is-now-available), which presumably can be used to run agents in the future.

**DevOps**

* **JFrog** [announced QFY25 earnings of  $136.9M ](https://s201.q4cdn.com/814780939/files/doc_financials/2025/q3/JFrog-Third-Quarter-2025-Financial-Results-Earnings-Call_Transcript.pdf)(+26% YoY), citing strong growth due to increased use of AI by their customers. 

* **Semaphore**, a CI/CD vendor, [released an MCP server](https://semaphore.io/blog/semaphore-mcp-server) available for free to all paying customers.

**Security**

* **Mend** [introduced a “risk reduction dashboard](https://www.mend.io/blog/introducing-the-mend-risk-reduction-dashboard/)” - an organisational level overview for security leaders. 

* The phased implementation of the Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification, used by the US DOD, began on November 10th, prompting new guidance from [**Sonatype**](https://www.sonatype.com/blog/cmmc-2.0-in-action-operationalizing-secure-software-practices-across-the-defense-industrial-base) and [**GitLab**](https://about.gitlab.com/blog/achieve-cmmc-level-2-fast-with-gitlab-dedicated-for-government/). Both had published guidance earlier this year (see Mona Monitor [17](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/24), [37](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/46)). 

* **Truffle Security**, the makers of TruffleHog, [raised a $25M series B](https://trufflesecurity.com/press/series-b), led by Intel Capital and A16Z

* **JFrog** [introduced an AI Generated Code Validation feature](https://jfrog.com/blog/jfrog-introduces-ai-generated-code-validation/).

**Data Points & Other**

* **JetBrains** provided a nice write up on the Go Ecosystem 

* **JellyFish**, a software engineering intelligence vendor, [published data](https://x.com/_jellyfish_co/status/1987903834501292276)showing Copilot Code Review was used by 68.7% of their customers using code review tools, far exceeding the next vendor, CodeRabbit at 12%.

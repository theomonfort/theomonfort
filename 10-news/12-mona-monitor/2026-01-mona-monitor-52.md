**Macro Trends and Emerging (Relevant) Tech**
_There is a robust ecosystem around GitHub that impacts our positioning and traction. This section brings select developments and pertinent analysis of technologies in our sphere._ 

* **NVIDIA** [entered into a licensing deal with **Groq**](https://groq.com/newsroom/groq-and-nvidia-enter-non-exclusive-inference-technology-licensing-agreement-to-accelerate-ai-inference-at-global-scale), a maker of inference focused chips, for $20Bn. Groq brings specialised software and compiler IP to NVIDIA. The majority of Groq’s team joins NVIDIA. Groq Cloud will continue as a separate entity. Unlike the similar licensing arrangement with **Windsurf** and **Google** last year (see Mona Monitor [31](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/40)), Groq employees were paid out on their vested stock with remaining options converted to NVIDIA stock. 

* **xAI**, makers of the Grok model, [announced a $20Bn Series E](https://x.ai/news/series-e), including investment from **NVIDIA** and **Cisco**.  

* **Marvell**, a chip maker, [acquired **XConn**](https://investor.marvell.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1004/marvell-to-acquire-xconn-technologies-expanding-leadership-in-ai-data-center-connectivity), a maker of interconnect switching technologies, for $540M.

* **Micron**, a memory manufacturer, [announced Q1FY26 earnings ](https://investors.micron.com/news-releases/news-release-details/micron-technology-inc-reports-results-first-quarter-fiscal-2026)of $13.64Bn (+56% YoY) driven by AI related demand.

**Competitor News**
_This section covers select updates and analysis from known and emerging competitors._  

**AI**

* **Cursor** [acquired **Graphite**](https://cursor.com/blog/graphite) for an undisclosed sum. Graphite will continue to operate independently for the early part of 2026, exact timelines have not been disclosed.  During a [TBPN interview](https://x.com/tbpn/status/2002092648207991221?s=20) both CEOs focused around collaboration as a core aspect of their future vision. Cursor CEO Michael Truell also stated that Cursor is present in 64% of the Fortune 500.  [ slack [thread](https://github.slack.com/archives/C1YRT029W/p1766161410056999) ]

* **Cursor** [announced a partner ecosystem for hooks](https://cursor.com/blog/hooks-partners), launch partners included [**SemGrep**](https://semgrep.dev/blog/2025/cursor-hooks-mcp-server/), [**Snyk**](https://snyk.io/blog/evo-agent-guard-cursor-integration/) and [**Endor Labs**](https://www.endorlabs.com/learn/bringing-malware-detection-into-ai-coding-workflows-with-cursor-hooks). 

* **Anthropic** ran a promotion from Dec 25th to Jan 31st offering double credits for usage of Claude Code.

* **WindSurf** [released Wave 13](https://windsurf.com/blog/windsurf-wave-13). The primary new feature is Git Worktree support. 

* **JetBrains** [announced the GA of their next edit suggestion capability](https://blog.jetbrains.com/ai/2025/12/next-edit-suggestions-now-generally-available/). JetBrains also [announced BYOK support](https://blog.jetbrains.com/ai/2025/12/bring-your-own-key-byok-is-now-live-in-jetbrains-ides/).

[**Cline**](https://cline.bot/blog/cline-3-47-0-adds-background-edits-and-a-free-minimax-2-1), [**Kilo Code**](https://blog.kilo.ai/p/minimax-m21-is-free-in-kilo-for-a) and [**Roocode**](https://x.com/roocode/status/2003367086593372189?s=20) all announced support with a limited free access period. We anticipate a significant number of models similar to MiniMax being released from China in the coming year.

**DevOps**

* **GitLab** [announced their 18.7 release](https://about.gitlab.com/releases/2025/12/18/gitlab-18-7-released/). GitLab are focused on their 18.8 release when Duo Agent Platform GAs, scheduled for January 2026, when Duo Agent Platform will GA. 18.7 includes false positive detection for their SAST offering and secret validity checks. 

* **Harness** [published a thought piece](https://www.harness.io/blog/knowledge-graph-rag) on their combination of a Knowledge Graph and RAG for AI DevOps. This mirrors similar language used by **GitLab** for their 18.4 release (see Mona Monitor [36](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/45) )

* **CircleCI** [now has support for keyless signing](https://circleci.com/blog/sign-oci-containers-and-other-artifacts-using-sigstore-cosign/) of container images and other artifacts, using Sigstore Fulcio. 

* **JetBrains** [announced TeamCity Pipelines is integrated as part of TeamCity Enterprise ](https://blog.jetbrains.com/teamcity/2025/12/pipelines-sunset/)and no longer available as a standalone product. Pipelines provides a visual editor for CI/CD pipelines, a product feature **Gartner** has looked for in previous DevOps Magic Quadrants (see Mona Monitor [36](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/45)).  

* **SourceGraph** are refocusing on SourceGraph Cloud, [announcing a plan for weekly updates ](https://webflow.sourcegraph.com/blog/weekly-updates-are-coming-to-sourcegraph-cloud)vs the monthly updates to their on-prem offering. This follows their experiment with their AI Coding Assistant Cody, followed by AMP, which has now been split off into a separate company (see Mona Monitor [41](https://github.com/github/Business-Insights/discussions/50)).

**Security**

* **Snyk**, who are heavily pushing AI-BOM as a concept, [announced ](https://snyk.io/blog/evo-adds-cyclonedx/)that Evo, their offering targeting AI Native Apps, is adopting CycloneDX as its BOM standard. 

* **Snyk** continued their long running string of announcements placing documentation on how to connect their MCP server as strategic integrations, this time with a [guide for **Augment Code**](https://docs.snyk.io/integrations/snyk-studio-agentic-integrations/quickstart-guides-for-snyk-studio/augment-code-guide). 

* **Mend** [announced](https://www.mend.io/blog/mend-leadership-update-the-next-phase-of-growth/) that co-founder Azi Choen has been appointed CEO. Former CEO Rami Saas is moving to a new role as GM for Mend AI. 

* **JetBrains** [announced an update to Qodana](https://blog.jetbrains.com/qodana/2025/12/top-3-qodana-2025-3-release-highlights/), their SAST offering, including additional license auditing capabilities for .NET.

**Data Points and Other**

* **JellyFish**, a software engineering intelligence vendor, [released their 2025 AI Metrics in Review report](https://jellyfish.co/blog/2025-ai-metrics-in-review/), which showed **Copilot** as the leading tool (56.1%), followed by **Claude Code** (38.6%) and then **Cursor** (28.5%). Jellyfish draw their data from enterprise customers paying for their solution.

* **LinearB**, a software engineering intelligence vendor, released their[ 2026 Software Engineering Benchmark Report](https://assets.linearb.io/image/upload/v1765300694/resources/LinearB_2026_Software_Engineering_Benchmarks_Report.pdf). Their sample size is 4800 development teams and 8.1M PRs. They note that AI-Assisted PRs are 2.6x the size of none AI-Assisted PRs. Agentic AI PRs take up to 5.2x longer to be reviewed (which seems understandable, but counter to the narrative LinearB desire), while they find that the merge rate for AI Assisted PRs is 32.7% vs 84.4% for non AI. 

* The **Eclipse Foundation** [published their 2026 predictions](https://eclipse-foundation.blog/2025/12/18/whats-in-store-for-open-source-in-2026/). Within the report the flagged that OpenVSX now has 110 million downloads a month, hosting 7,000 extensions from approximately 5000 publishers. 

* Analyst firm **RedMonk** published “[10 Things Developers Want from their Agentic IDES in 2025](https://redmonk.com/kholterhoff/2025/12/22/10-things-developers-want-from-their-agentic-ides-in-2025/)”. Copilot implements almost all of these already, we just need to remind RedMonk that there is life, and product documentation, beyond reddit and X.
